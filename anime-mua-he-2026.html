Summer 2026 arrives with an anime lineup that will make any fan look at the schedule and reconsider their viewing priorities. Beyond sequels to top-tier series, there are exciting new titles from notable studios and authors. OtaHub rounds up the 10 series you cannot miss this season.

1. Chainsaw Man Season 3 (October)

Expected to be the anime peak of 2026. MAPPA continues the Aging Devil arc with a 40% budget increase and Fujimoto's direct oversight. If you have been waiting since Season 2 ended, this is what you waited for. See OtaHub's dedicated Chainsaw Man S3 article for full details.

2. Dungeon Meshi Season 2

Delicious in Dungeon Season 1 ended with very high Crunchyroll and MyAnimeList scores. Season 2 continues Laios and company deeper into the dungeon as the story begins getting darker in exactly the way Ryoko Kui intended in the manga. Studio Trigger continues production with the same team. Summer 2026 is officially the season of monster cooking.

3. Frieren: Beyond Journey's End Part 2

Frieren is the first anime to win over manga fans, isekai fans and casual viewers simultaneously. The continuation arrives in summer and continues the First Class Mage Exam arc, where the magic competitions showcase Kanehito Yamada's subtle storytelling. Part 2 may be where viewers encounter the series' most impressive characters alongside Frieren herself.

4. Kaiju No. 8 Season 2

Season 1 of Kaiju No. 8 ended with Kafka Hibino facing the defense force he most wants to protect. Season 2 reveals more about the origins of Kaiju No. 8, concepts that will overturn some viewer assumptions. Production I.G, with their strong sci-fi action pedigree, is a solid quality guarantee.

5. Oshi no Ko Season 3

Part 3 of Oshi no Ko enters its most challenging arc: the 2.5D Stage Play arc, where the story critically examines how Japan's entertainment industry handles personal diaries, memoirs and public performance. This manga arc is highly rated but difficult to adapt as it must convey both comedy and deep contemplation simultaneously. Doga Kobo has the toughest assignment this season.

6. Dan Da Dan Season 2

Science SARU and Netflix continue Dan Da Dan with the same energy as Season 1. The next arc features Okarun encountering even stranger creatures while his feelings for Momo become clearer. Science SARU's distinctive visual style remains one of the most interesting things happening in anime right now.

7. Isekai Ojisan Part 2

Uncle from Another World finally returns after Atelier Pontdarc's production difficulties. Part 2 expands on the uncle's time in the other world and his comically difficult adjustment to modern life. Critics call it "the most well-crafted isekai comedy currently airing" and Part 2 looks to continue that trajectory.

8. Sakamoto Days (TV Animation)

Following the excellent manga arc on Jump+, Netflix is producing a TV animation series featuring Sakamoto Days protagonist Taro Sakamoto, an extraordinarily powerful retired assassin. This legendary action-comedy is expected to satisfy those who already burned through the manga.

9. The Apothecary Diaries Season 2

Kusuriya no Hitorigoto Season 2 continues Maomao's adventures in a fictional ancient Chinese imperial court. Season 1 ended with many mysteries unresolved, and Season 2 promises to go deeper into the complex political relationships the series is renowned for handling with such finesse.

10. NieR: Automata The Animation - Long-Awaited Sequel Arc

NieR:Automata Ver1.1a Part 2 finally confirmed its premiere date after extended delays. The A2 arc and the revelation of 2B's true fate are what fans have been waiting for since the abrupt interruption. Aniplex promises this will be the series' darkest arc.
